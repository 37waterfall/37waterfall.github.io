选择“热门和冷门专业”

  • 准大学生
  • 分析利弊
  • 只有兴趣-偏题
Choosing a college major is never easy.

 

Students interested in certain unpopular majors may be hesitant about choosing them,
especially when they have far more popular options that will probably bring them a lucrative career.

If I were them, I would give priority to my own interests.

 

The biggest reason for my decision is that interest will play a critical role in keeping me
motivated to acquire whatever knowledge and skills relevant to my major.

 

In other words, one will easily become a hard worker, and ultimately be well qualified
for a satisfactory job, once he can get enormous pleasure from what he is studying.

 

Moreover, popular majors are often highly competitive, which means a much higher risk of
being rejected, especially those in prestigious universities.

When it comes to picking a major, blindly following latest trends does not necessarily
ensure a promising future, as the world is changing so rapidly that what seems popular
or in great need at the moment my not be so several years later.

 

In general, what maters most is how much effort one would like to put in his major,
which largely depends on whether it is interesting enough for him.

002

天赋和努力

  • 点出主题-引用格言
  • 阐述观点-分析利弊
  • 总结-努力更重要
Which one is more important, hard work or talent?

 

People always hold widely divergent views on this subject.

 

But no one would ever deny that hard workers, even with less natural talent,
are a force to be reckoned with.

Those endowed with an enviable ability do have an advantage over ordinary people.

 

However, they may soon be surpassed by highly motivated hard workers if they
don’t make a good use of it.

 

In other words, talent is at best a keen eye for the key to success, as opposed to the
key itself, the lack of which can be made up for by diligence to some extent.

 

This explains why it’s not unusual for an underdog who trains really hard day in
and day out to upset a more gifted opponent whose heart is simply not in this sport.

Hard work pays off, while a waste of talent leads to mediocrity.

 

That’s what the saying “hard work beats talent when talent doesn’t work hard” means exactly.

 

Not everyone is born a genius, but at least we all have the right to choose whether
to make persistent efforts.
